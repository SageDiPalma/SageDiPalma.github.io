BIO

Hello and welcome!
My name is Sage DiPalma. I am a classically trained operatic coloratura soprano, instrumentalist, composer, and sound engineer who has decided to delve into the world of tech! I am based in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania.

I began studying classical guitar and piano around age 5, then lessons in classical voice at age 8. While growing up in a musical family, I often had to go on the road and assist my parents with carrying amps, mics, monitors, and cables, even plugging in and setting up equipment as well. When in the music studio, I would gasp in awe over the 48-channel mixing console in front of my eyes. And while I would enjoy the music, I always wanted to be part of the planning, recording, and behind-the-scenes work that made it possible for the musicians to record.

My interest and desire began to shift into actionable experiences when I was an assistant to another musician, audio engineer, and (ironically) software developer during my teen years. There was a project to build, not use, but build a MOTM modular Moog analog synthesizer and I was uniquely given the task of assisting with this! So rather than just utilizing a great piece of machinery as a tool for enjoyment, I was using tools to create this machinery that would bring enjoyment as well as the satisfaction and sense of accomplishment that came with that. This was when I realized I enjoy difficult tasks and the value of skills like patience, perseverance, creativity, and the ‘beginner’s mind’ so that you always stay willing to open your mind to learn and grow.

While in college, I was studying operatic voice and performing. Outside of my major, I studied audio engineering with a mentor and received some formal training as well. I was working with musicians on different aspects of their career development; From recording, to songwriting and utilizing web templates to provide other musicians and peers performer websites to appeal to clients, management, and the public. As a performer, It is very important to have your own website if you want to showcase your music, performance media, and press kits.

The year that I graduated college I opened a storefront with a few friends to provide local Philadelphians with tech, video, audio, and repair services and products. My specialty was music hardware and software. Simultaneously working at a music studio and performing opera, my life was immersed in all things music! Philadelphia, being a city with a thriving arts and culture community, benefited from many of the services we provided.

However, as the Covid-19 pandemic hit, there became an increased need to utilize services for online presence for performers and local companies to build and maintain their online presence in order to continue the transition. This pivotal moment for so many became my foundation for expanding my abilities so that I could become useful and relevant to the change that so many people and industries were experiencing. While in the tech business, I have seen how hardware devices have only become better with the increased enhancement in software and the cloud world! Which brings me to today, I look forward to utilizing the wonderful opportunity in my daily life.
